Scott Pilgrim


Out titular 22 year-old hero. Well..."hero" might be too strong a word: one of the first things we see him do in book 1 is cheat on poor Knives! Although he starts (and let's be honest, ends) the novels as not the greatest person in the world, his clear love for Ramona and acts throughout the series easily redeem him in our eyes

Ramona Flowers


Just who is Ramona Flowers? This is a question that gets asked a lot during the series, and by the end of the book we're not really closer to any answers. What we can say is that she's one of the most iconic female comic book characters for severl reasons: She's funny, kicks ass, and has a magical handbag that acts as a portal to her own head.

Wallace Wells


Ah Wallace, where do we begin? Wallace's cool, calm, and sarcastic demenour is the perfect counterbalance to Scott's nervous energy. He has arguably the most funny one-liners in the entire series and is a delight whenever he's on the page. Also did you know he's gay???

Kim Pine


"If your life had a face, I'd punch it in the balls" is one famous line that Kim says to Scott early in the series, and this sums up her character perfectly. She's the "moody" one of the group, but to chalk her up to just that is to miss all of her deep character traits and surprisingly touching backstory with Scott. Also who can forget "WE ARE SEX BOB-OMB! 1 2 3 4!"

Steven Stills


Is Steven the only normal character out of the main cast? It sure seems like it at times when th rest of the gang if fighting in libraries or destroying supermarkets with vegan powers. All he wants to do is make an album but stuff keeps getting in the way! We feel for you Steven, we really do.

Young Neil


Young Neil seems to only exists to provide occasional exposition and to fill in certain plot necessaties, but you know what? That's OK. His name is on of the bets running joke sin the series and the payoff from that in book 6 is incredible. I don't know where he's going after the end of the series, but I hope it's far, I really do.

Envy Adams


We hear about Envy way before we actually meet her in book 3: after all, her and Scott's breakup is one of the biggest reasons that Scott is the way he is. She's painted as a villain through and through but by the end of the series you realise that that isn't the case, and that a certain Mr Pilgrim is unreliable in his recollection of events.

Knives Chau


Knives is such an innocent character who you feel sorry for at the beggining of the series. First of all she falls head-over-heels for Scott , then she gets cheated on, then her favourite artist is a terrible person, then her dad tries to kill the boy she's pining over. She wouldn't hurt a fly. I mean, except for the time she tries to kill rmaona in cold blood like. Wallace was right though, she deserved better than Scott.
